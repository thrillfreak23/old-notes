** Basic ideas of Rago
*** based on predicates
**** li: predicate marker
**** everything after the li in a sentence is the predicate
**** predicates are formed by li + a predicant
**** a predicant is any content word
*** open class modifiers go after what they modify
**** a predicant in the modifier position becomes an adjective
**** so does everything else
*** closed class modifiers go before what they modify
*** words are invariant other than a couple of suffixes like -li
*** 2 prepositions: ana for relations that are not permanent, kata for permanent relations
**** this is like long and blong in Tok Pisin

* <<Predicants>>: how they work

** some predicants:
- luba
  - luba li bon: luba = noun
  - me li luba: luba = verb
- toki
  - toki li bon: toki = noun
  - me li toki: toki = verb
- rago
  - rago li bon: rago = noun
  - me li rago: rago = verb (speak rago)
- lumo
  - lumo li bon: lumo = noun
  - sol li lumo: lumo = verb or noun
    - The sun is a light.
    - The sun is-bright.
- mute
  - mute li bon: mute = noun, bigness or a big thing
    - Big is good.  Being big is a good thing.  
  - me li mute: mute = noun or adjective
    - I am a big thing.
    - I am big.

- maje
  : similarly with min and sama
  - maje li bon: more is good
  - mute maje li bon: bigger is good
  - mute maje li bon maje: bigger is better
  - en chi li mute maje kom en cha : this thing is bigger than that thing
  - en meli li mute maje kom en tuli: my thing is bigger than your thing

** kio tu li ana domo?
- Are you at home?
- ana isn't a predicant by itself, it just needs a complement like domo here to be a predicant
- ana domo is a predicate meaning "be at home"

Pabo li kanem. Pabo li nos pi kanem. Pabo li nos pi kanem bon. Pabo li kanem tat nos li luba e lo.
Pablo is a dog.  Pablo is our dog.  Pablo is our good dog.  Pablo is the dog that we love.

rago la, "li" li meto e chi: blah
in the context of Rago, "li" means, amounts to, is defined as this: blah

meto: predicant: amounts to, be equal or equivalent to, is defined as, to mean
Rachel li me pi nomen.  Rachel li meto e me.  Rachel is my name. Rachel means me.

tempo chi li nubeli: literally, this instance is cloudy
meaning the conditions now are cloudy, meaning the weather
tempo: conditions, situation, the way things are; the weather; the "climate", metaphorically
